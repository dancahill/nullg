12 - Orders

12.1 - Listing Orders

          When you first enter the orders section, you will be presented with a form for searching for orders. On the left side of the form is an option box which will allow you either search for a match in all fields, or just a selected field. The textbox allows you to specify a string of text to search for. This can be the order id, a name, or anything else you wish to search for. Wildcards such as * and ? can be used in searches. The button on the right is used to submit the search. Searches are case insensitive.

          If a search returns more than 50 results, then the output will be broken up into pages, each page having 50 or less results.

          If you are unfamiliar with wildcards, a quick summary is as follows. The '*' character can be substituted in a search string for any number of characters. The '?' character can be substituted for only one. For example, a search for "*" (the default) will return all records. A search for "*1*" would return all records that have a 1 anywhere in them. Searching for "1?" would return only records which have a matching field two characters long, with the first one being a 1.

          After submitting a search, a list of all matches will be returned. This list will show the order id, customer name, order date, amount due and amount received. Clicking on the order id number will bring up a view of that order. Clicking on the customer name will bring up a view of the contact.

    Viewing Orders

          Order views can be accessed by several means, two of which are from contact order list, and of course, the order search/list. Both the viewer and the editor display the same information, however the editor can be used to add and edit orders.

    Order Date

          The 'Order Date' field is the date on which the order was originally created.

    Customer

          The 'Customer' field is the name of the customer associated with this order. When working with the order system, the words 'customer' and 'contact' may be used interchangeably. All customers should have an entry in the contact section.

    Employee

          The 'Employee' field is used to record the username of the person who originally created this order.

    Order Type

          The 'Order Type' field is used to indicate the nature of this order. Possible entries include 'Sale', 'AR Payment' (Accounts Receivable) and 'Quote'.

    Payment Method

          The 'Payment Method' field is used to indicate the means of payment used for this order. Possible entries include 'Cash', 'Cheque', 'Credit Card', 'Debit Card' and 'Money Order'.

    Payment Due

          The 'Payment Due' field is the sum amount due for all items in this order, including applicable taxes.

    Payment Received

          The 'Payment Received' field is the total payment made on this order.

    Details

          The 'Details' field is used to record any additional details for anything which may not be clear in the invoice and/or any reasons for changing the order information at a later date.

    Order Items

          The 'Order Items' list displays all items or products which have been added to this order. Each item entry shows the product name, the quantity, any discount, the base price of the product, PST, and GST. Order items can be added or edited by clicking on 'Add Item' or 'edit' respectively. Items can be deleting by clicking on 'edit' and then 'Delete'.

    Notes on Taxes

          The default taxes, PST (Provincial Sales Tax) and GST (Goods and Services Tax) may have the wrong percentages for your region, or may be entirely the wrong taxes for your country. The names and default percentages of both taxes can be changed by modifying the corresponding entries in the 'gw_dbinfo' table.

12.2 - Adding and Editing Orders

          When adding a new order, you will first be asked for the customer id. If the name of the customer does not appear on the list, then you may have to add this customer as a new contact in the contact section. Once the contact info is provided, you may create an order for this customer. Choose the name of the customer from the list, and click on save. A new order will be created.

          Now that you've created a new order, you can add items to the order. Click on 'Add Item' to add a new item to the list. You will be prompted for the product name, and the quantity. If the requested product is not on the list, and you do not have an entry for 'miscellaneous', then you may have to add the product to the database before this order can be completed.

          Adding the new item will bring you back to the order view form. The new item you just added will be listed at the bottom of the page. If you need to change any details for this item (e.g. to change the quantity or the discount), you can do so by clicking 'edit' beside the item.

          Continue adding items until all requested items are on the order. Once this is done, click 'edit' at the top of the order form, and confirm that all fields are filled in correctly. Check the order type and payment method to make sure they are correct, and change the 'Payment Received' field to reflect any payment which has been made for this invoice. If there are any notes or comments to be attached to this order, now would be a good time to add them in the 'Invoice Notes' box. Click on 'Save' and your order is done.

12.3 - Adding and Editing Products

    Listing Products

          A complete listing of all products in the database can be accessed by clicking on 'VIEW PRODUCTS' from any page in the order system. In the left column of the list will be the product name. Clicking on the product name will take you to a view of the product's information. In the second column of the product list will be the category of the product. In the third column will be the unmodified base unit price of the product.

          At the bottom of the products list will be a link 'New Product' which will allow you to add a new product.

    Viewing Products

          The product view and edit forms display the exact same information. Both forms include the following fields.

    Product Name

          The 'Product Name' field is the short descriptive name that will be used to identify this product (or service) in most order views.

    Category

          The 'Category' field indicates the general class of product or service under which this item falls. This field is used as a user guide, and has no effect on the function of the order system.

    Discount

          The 'Discount' field indicates whether any discount should be assigned to this product. If it is non-zero, future sales of this product will receive the indicated discount. Note that the number shown on this form is the decimal notation of the discount percentage (e.g. 50% will appear as 0.50).

    Unit Price

          The 'Unit Price' field is the base unit price (before discounts and taxes) of this product for one unit.

    Internal Cost

          The 'Internal Cost' field can be used for accounting purposes to calculate net profit for a sale (e.g. Unit Price - Internal Cost = Profit). This value is not used in the processing of a customer invoice.

    PST (Provincial Sales Tax)

          The 'PST' field is used to indicate the percentage of the unit price to charge for tax 1. As with the 'Discount' field, this field is in decimal notation. 8% tax would be entered as 0.08.

    GST (Goods and Services Tax)

          The 'GST' field is used to indicate the percentage of the unit price to charge for tax 2. As with the 'Discount' field, this field is in decimal notation. 8% tax would be entered as 0.08.

    Notes on Taxes

          The default taxes, PST (Provincial Sales Tax) and GST (Goods and Services Tax) may have the wrong percentages for your region, or may be entirely the wrong taxes for your country. The names and default percentages of both taxes can be changed by modifying the corresponding entries in the 'gw_dbinfo' table.
